About

I'm a Mechatronics Engineering student at Simon Fraser University, working at the intersection of embedded firmware, robotics, and hardware that has to survive contact with the real world.

My path here started under the hood of cars, not in a classroom. Rebuilding engines, diagnosing hybrid battery packs, and machining custom ignition coils taught me how mechanical systems actually fail and why they work when they do. That hands‑on instinct followed me into formal engineering: at Team Phantom, SFU's Formula SAE Electric team, I led the electric subsystem as Electric Team Lead, redesigning power‑delivery architecture and writing the control logic that decided when the car got its boost.

These days my focus has shifted toward software that runs closer to the metal — ROS2 nodes, C++, control loops, computer vision — but the underlying question hasn't changed: how does this system behave under real conditions, and what's the cleanest way to make it reliable. I like engineering best when it's a little bit like poetry: every design choice is a line, and the discipline is in making each one earn its place.

Projects

Five builds that taught me the most, in roughly chronological order.

HiWonder ROS2 Robot

ROS2 · OpenCV · RViz · rqt

Built the full software stack for a HiWonder robot: ROS2 nodes for servo control and sensor processing, a transmitter/receiver integration for remote operation, and an OpenCV pipeline for autonomous tape‑following. The hardest part was coordinating the arm, gripper, and camera closely enough to reliably pick up chess pieces — which made clean node architecture a necessity, not a nice‑to‑have.

Rebuilding a Prius Hybrid Battery

High-voltage safety · battery diagnostics

Pulled a failed hybrid pack from a parts‑only Gen 1 Prius, diagnosed all 38 NiMH modules individually, replaced the weak cells with salvaged good ones, and rebalanced the pack. After reassembly and torque‑spec'd insulation work, the car cleared its warning lights and returned to normal hybrid operation.

Custom Ignition Coils

SolidWorks · CNC · ECU tuning

Designed upgraded ignition coils for a VW Golf 2.5L inline‑five, working out winding ratios and core materials by hand, then partnering with a machine shop to manufacture the housings to spec. Final assembly, insulation, and ECU ignition‑timing adjustments were mine — which pushed me deeper into automotive embedded systems.

Arduino PID Temperature Controller

Closed-loop control · PID tuning

Wired a thermistor, potentiometer, and LCD to an Arduino, then wrote the control code to read the sensor, run a PID loop, and drive a heater over PWM. Most of the real work was tuning P, I, and D by hand while watching live feedback until the system stopped overshooting and held its setpoint.

Go-Kart Build

Mechanical design · engine rebuild

Built a go‑kart from scratch with no formal training — just everything I'd picked up fixing cars. Rebuilt a Honda GX160 engine, sorted out suspension geometry and steering alignment, and assembled the drivetrain. This was the project that made me realize I wanted to do this for a living.
